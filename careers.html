Common questions

Straight answers about studying on OpenLearn and what we offer today.

Do I need prior experience to start?

No. Paths like Frontend Developer and Python Developer start from foundations. If you already code, jump to intermediate courses via the course catalog sidebar.

How long should I study each path?

It depends on your pace and how much time you have. Most paths suggest roughly 6–16 weeks of focused study. Consistency matters more than speed — even a few hours a week adds up.

Is OpenLearn free?

Yes — you can explore all guided paths and courses at no cost. We believe access to structured learning should not depend on your budget.

How is this different from YouTube tutorials?

Tutorials teach random topics. OpenLearn gives you ordered paths — what to study first, what comes next, and how lessons connect toward a career-shaped goal.

Does OpenLearn help me get a job?

OpenLearn is for learning — courses, paths, and practice. We do not list jobs or handle applications. This page helps you choose what to study toward the kind of work you are interested in.

What does the Careers page mean then?

It shows study paths inspired by common tech roles — so you know which courses to take if you are learning toward frontend work, Python, full-stack, and similar goals.

Ready to start studying?

Pick a path, open the courses, and learn at your own pace. That is what OpenLearn is for today.
